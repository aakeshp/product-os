# Attributions

The Product OS synthesises thinking from a range of practitioners, researchers, and bodies of work. This file maintains attribution transparently — both for intellectual honesty and to help users go deeper on specific ideas.

This is a living document. As the OS develops and draws on new sources, they are added here.

---

## Core influences

### AI-native product management practice
**Tal Raviv** — Substack (talraviv.co), Maven workshops, Lenny's Newsletter guest posts  
Raviv's work on building AI-native PM systems — using the same tools as engineers, developing genuine AI product sense through practice, and building personal productivity systems with agents — directly informs the OS's approach to agentic workflows and the operational layer. His framing of the PM who builds real AI systems rather than just using AI tools is a design principle throughout.  
*Where referenced:* Operational layer, agentic engineering practices, principal-os design  
[talraviv.co](https://www.talraviv.co)

### Evidence-guided product management
**Itamar Gilad** — *Evidence Guided* (book), GIST framework, Confidence Meter  
The principle that product decisions should be grounded in a continuously updated evidence base, with explicit confidence levels, is central to the OS. Gilad's confidence scoring approach directly informs the beliefs layer of the Knowledge Core and the Decision Layer methodology.  
*Where referenced:* Knowledge Core beliefs layer, confidence scoring, Decision Layer  
[itamargilad.com](https://itamargilad.com)

### Continuous discovery
**Teresa Torres** — *Continuous Discovery Habits* (book), producttalk.org  
The practice of weekly user touchpoints as a habit rather than a periodic research project is drawn from Torres's continuous discovery framework. The opportunity mapping practice also builds on her work.  
*Where referenced:* Discovery Engine — continuous touchpoints, opportunity mapping  
[producttalk.org](https://producttalk.org)

### User interview technique
**Rob Fitzpatrick** — *The Mom Test*  
The principle that good user research focuses on past behaviour and specific experiences rather than opinions and hypotheticals informs the touchpoint synthesis prompt design.  
*Where referenced:* Discovery Engine — touchpoint synthesis prompt

### Systems thinking in product management
**John Cutler** — Substack (cutlefish.substack.com), Mind the Product, various writing  
Cutler's work on systems thinking in product — understanding feedback loops, organisational conditions for good product work, and treating internal processes as products — informs the cross-squad and platform-level thinking in the OS. Particularly relevant to the Knowledge Core's synthesis layer and how a Principal PM maintains a system-level view across squads. His framing that we should be as rigorous about internal systems as we are about customer systems is a design principle throughout.  
*Where referenced:* Knowledge Core — squads layer, synthesis layer, Principal PM context  
[cutlefish.substack.com](https://cutlefish.substack.com)

## How to add to this file

When a specific methodology, practice, or prompt draws meaningfully on external work, add an entry here following the format above. Include:

- **Who** — name and source
- **What** — the specific idea or practice
- **Where** — which part of the OS it influences
- **Link** — where to go deeper

Attribution should be specific enough to be useful — not just "influenced by" but "this specific practice is drawn from this specific work."

---

## A note on synthesis

The Product OS does not implement any single framework wholesale. It synthesises across sources, adapts for AI-native workflows and public sector contexts, and adds practices developed through direct experience. Where we adapt rather than directly derive, we note the influence without claiming the adapted work is the original author's.

If you believe something in this OS should carry attribution that isn't currently noted, please raise it.
